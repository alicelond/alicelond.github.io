About

Hello there, I'm Alice Becker Londero, an acoustical and software engineer with a passion for building software that bring solutions connecting the physical and digital worlds. Currently, I'm a Master's candidate in Computer Science at the University of São Paulo (USP), where I focus on digital processing techniques to process vibration data on real-time systems.

As someone that is led by curiosity, my profissional carrer has been driven by experimentation where I worked in different fields in Acoustical Engineering, which include room acoustics, building acoustics, environmental acoustics and digital signal processing. A common factor in all these experiences was that I always looked for challenges and used code to ease processes which has lead me to software engineering.

By increasing my computing skills, I was able to build code and solve problems that helped other people understand better complex physical systems, which has amazed me. On this blog, "From signal to software," I document what intrigues me: from how engineering devices from our daily lives are made, what I learn from the world of software engineering and reflections from the tech topics that affects everyone that work in this field.

Description: Used machine learning to estimate train velocity using vibration signals captured by accelerometers.

Technologies: Scikit-learn, Scipy, Librosa, Pandas, Numpy

Key features and metrics: Achieved a 45% reduction in Mean Absolute Error (MAE) compared to the baseline, from 10.85 km/h to 5.93 km/h, and engineered 42 features from raw vibration data.
